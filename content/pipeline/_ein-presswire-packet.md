## ② Summary / Subheadline（任意・1〜2文）

## ③ Dateline + Body（ここから下を丸ごとコピペ）

TOKYO, June 5, 2026 /EIN Presswire/ -- Fureaino-Oka (operated by Kogera Inc.), a Japanese information media on end-of-life home organizing (seizen-seiri) and vacant-home ("akiya") issues, has published a free, openly licensed dataset on municipal demolition subsidies for vacant and aging homes across Japan. The study covers 1,726 of Japan's 1,741 municipalities (about 99%; source: Japan's Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, "Statistical Observations of Municipalities 2025").

Key findings:

- A vacant-home demolition subsidy could be confirmed in 844 of the 1,726 surveyed municipalities (48.9%). Roughly half of all municipalities have no confirmable program.
- Among the 532 municipalities where a maximum subsidy amount could be quantified, the median cap was 500,000 yen (approx. US$3,200), with an average of about 644,000 yen.
- The highest cap was 15.5 million yen in Shinagawa City, Tokyo, reflecting special programs in dense urban fire-prevention districts.
- To the best of our knowledge, no nationwide statistic on "the share of municipalities offering such subsidies" had been publicly available; this study provides that primary data.

Note on methodology: "confirmed" means the program's existence was verified in official public information. Municipalities where a program could not be confirmed are not necessarily without one.

Behind the data is a growing household concern: when an inherited family home is designated a "specified vacant house," it can lose Japan's residential-land property-tax reduction, sharply raising the owner's tax burden. Demolition, however, costs anywhere from several hundred thousand to several million yen, and subsidy availability, caps, and conditions vary widely by municipality — creating strong demand for a single, comparable reference.

The dataset was compiled and normalized from official municipal websites, with every record linked to its source URL. It is free to use in articles, reporting, research, and public materials with attribution (CC BY 4.0). Open data in CSV/JSON, ready-to-paste citation text, and chart visuals are also provided.
